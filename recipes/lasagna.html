Lasagna:

What's the Lasagna?

Lasagna is an Italian dish made of layers of pasta, sauce, cheese

and usually meat or vegetables baked together in the oven until it's hot, cheesy, and delicious!

Lasagna is usually made with:

  • Flat, wide pasta sheets
  • Tomato sauce (sometimes with ground beef or sausage)
  • Ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese
  • Herbs like basil and oregano
  • Sometimes vegetables like spinach, mushrooms, or zucchini

Instructions for making the Lasagna:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook ground beef, then mix in tomato sauce and seasonings.
  2. Layer a baking dish with sauce, lasagna noodles, ricotta cheese, and mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layers.
  3. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then remove foil and bake for another 20 minutes.
  4. Let it cool for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
